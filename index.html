Welcome to My Body My Rights!

This website provides information of abortion laws and restrictions in the United States, and locations of abortion clinics in each state (including the District of Columbia and Puerto Rico). We also provide relevant news articles, information on adoption resources and pro-choice organizations that welcome donations. Our goal is to provide easily accessible and factual information for those seeking out or considering abortions.

Alabama

Abortion Rights in Alabama:

- Pre-viability: no restrictions before 20 weeks

- Post-viability: legal if recipient's health or life is in immediate danger

- Partial Birth Abortions: illegal

- Telemedicine: not permitted

- Waiting Period: 48 hours after state anti-abortion counseling

- Consent: one parental consent if under 18 unless court-ordered

- Additional: abortions can only be performed by someone with a state license in medicine or osteopathy; healthcare plans and public funds only cover abortions for rape, incest, or to save recipient's life

- Pending Legislation: restrictive laws in which all abortions would be illegal except those needed to save the recipient's life

This map illustrates our rating scale of which states have the harshest abortion laws.
Dark = States with Most Restrictive Laws; Pale = States with Least Restrictive Laws

How did we rate the states for this map?

To come up with a comprehensive rating scale, we used a point system based on how many restrictions states had implemented. We used seven categories of restrictions to rate each state: telemedicine, pre-viability restrictions, post-viability restrictions, restrictions on partial birth abortions, ultrasound requirements, counseling requirements, waiting periods, and consent requirements.

For example, Idaho received a score of 5 because of its moderately restrictive laws:
post-viability restrictions (2 points) + counseling requirements (1 point) + waiting period (1 point) + consent requirement (1 point) = 5 points
